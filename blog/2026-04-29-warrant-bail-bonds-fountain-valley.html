If you just discovered there's a warrant out for you in Fountain Valley — or someone you love is in that situation — the worst move is to wait. Warrants in California don't expire. They sit in the OC Sheriff's database and the statewide CLETS system indefinitely until cleared. Any traffic stop, employment background check, or fingerprint-based gun purchase can trigger an arrest. This guide explains what kind of warrant you're dealing with, how to clear it, and the Fountain Valley-specific booking and court process.

Two Types of Warrants — Why It Matters

Arrest warrant: Issued by a judge based on probable cause that someone committed a NEW crime — typically at the request of a prosecutor or investigating officer. Examples: assault charges, drug offenses, fraud allegations.

Bench warrant: Issued by a judge from the bench (in court) for non-compliance with a court order — failure to appear at a scheduled hearing, failure to pay fines, missed probation check-ins, missed required classes.

Both authorize law enforcement to take the person into custody. The bail amount, court process, and severity of consequences differ significantly. Knowing which type you have determines the right strategy.

How to Check If You Have a Warrant in Fountain Valley

  1. Call the Orange County Superior Court clerk: Westminster Courthouse handles most Fountain Valley cases at (657) 622-5400. Provide your full name and date of birth and ask if a warrant is active.
  2. OC Court Online Services: Visit occourts.org → "Online Services" → "Case Search," and enter your case number if you have it.
  3. Hire a criminal defense attorney: Attorneys can perform a confidential warrant check via court records access without exposing you to arrest. Safest option if there's an active criminal case.

Don't walk into the courthouse to "just ask." Courthouse security or sheriff's deputies posted at the courthouse can arrest you on the spot if a warrant is active. Always call first or go through an attorney.

Why Voluntary Surrender Is the Smart Move

  • Custody time is shorter — walk-ins often spend hours instead of days. The bond is pre-approved, paperwork is ready
  • Judges view it favorably — voluntary appearance demonstrates accountability. Many judges reduce bail amounts or quash bench warrants entirely on minor charges
  • You avoid the worst-case — being arrested at home, work, or during a traffic stop in front of family is traumatic. You're processed without your attorney, bondsman, or support system ready
  • Background-check fallout is contained — an arrest record from a planned surrender looks very different from a "fugitive from justice" notation

What Bail to Expect

For warrants with bail amounts attached:

  • Bench warrant on misdemeanor traffic / fee non-payment: $500-$5,000
  • Bench warrant on misdemeanor failure-to-appear: $5,000-$25,000
  • Arrest warrant on misdemeanor charge: $5,000-$50,000
  • Arrest warrant on felony charge: $25,000-$250,000+ (often requires judge's hearing)

The 10% premium under California Insurance Code §1800.4 means $50-$25,000+ paid to the bondsman depending on the bail amount. Payment plans available for higher amounts.

Frequently Asked Questions

Arrest warrant vs bench warrant?
Arrest warrant = new crime authorization (signed at prosecutor or officer request). Bench warrant = court-order non-compliance (failure to appear, missed fines, missed check-ins). Both stay active until cleared.
How do I check if I have a warrant?
Call Westminster Courthouse clerk at (657) 622-5400, search OC Court Online Services at occourts.org, or hire a criminal defense attorney for a confidential check. Don't walk into the courthouse to ask.
Can I bail out on a warrant?
Yes — most warrants have a bail amount. Angels Bail Bonds can post the surety bond at FVPD Jail or wherever the defendant is held. Coordinate before walking in.
Why turn myself in voluntarily?
Faster processing, shorter custody, judges look favorably. Pre-coordinated bond can drop custody from days to hours.
Where do Fountain Valley warrant arrests get booked?
FVPD Jail (10200 Slater Ave) initially, then OC Intake/Release Center within 24-48 hours. Cases heard at Westminster Courthouse.
