Where Fountain Valley Arrests Are Processed

Someone arrested in Fountain Valley is transported to the Fountain Valley Police Department Jail at 10200 Slater Avenue, Fountain Valley, CA 92708 (phone: 714-593-4485). The FVPD Jail is co-located with the Fountain Valley Police Department headquarters near the Fountain Valley Civic Center, adjacent to the 405/22/55 freeway triangle.

For serious felony charges or when the city facility is at capacity, defendants transfer to the Orange County Intake and Release Center (IRC) at 550 N Flower Street, Santa Ana, CA 92703. This is the intake point for the Orange County Sheriff's Department (OCSD) jail system, which also includes the Theo Lacy Facility in Orange and the James A. Musick Facility in Irvine. Fountain Valley in Detail — What Makes This City's Bail Cases Different

Fountain Valley has its own police department and a city jail at 10200 Slater Avenue, adjacent to Fountain Valley City Hall and the Civic Center. FVPD handles local bookings, and the jail is sized for a mid-volume city of 56,000 — meaning most misdemeanor cases process locally before any transfer decision is made.

The geography is what drives the arrest pattern. Fountain Valley sits inside the 405-22-55 freeway triangle, with the 405 forming the southern boundary, the 22 the northern, and the 55 the eastern. Three freeway interchanges in a compact city mean heavy commuter volume and reliable DUI enforcement on all three corridors — especially Friday and Saturday nights between 10 PM and 2 AM. Beyond DUIs, the city's commercial zones (Fountain Valley Crossings near the 405, and the Euclid/Warner strip) generate retail-theft and occasional robbery calls.

The relationship to Orange County IRC matters here. A misdemeanor bond posted at FVPD usually releases within 1–3 hours. A felony or overflow case transfers to the Orange County IRC at 550 N Flower Street in Santa Ana — a 10-minute drive — and the release window stretches to 4–8 hours. The tactical play for families is always to post at FVPD before transport when the charge permits. Angels Bail Bonds monitors the transfer window directly through FVPD's booking desk.

How a Fountain Valley Bail Bond Actually Works

You call (626) 478-1062. We confirm the bail amount from the Orange County bail schedule — fountain valley bail bonds typically range from $5,000 for petty theft (PC 484) to $50,000 for domestic violence (PC 273.5) and $100,000 for DUI with injury (VC 23153). You pay Angels Bail Bonds a 10% premium — the state-set rate under California Insurance Code § 1800.4. We post the full bail amount with the jail as a surety bond. The defendant is released, typically 1–4 hours at FVPD Jail and 4–8 hours at the Orange County Intake and Release Center (IRC).

The 10% is a fee for services — it is a premium paid to the bondsman, not a loan. When the case concludes and all court appearances are made, the bond is exonerated.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where does a Fountain Valley arrest actually get processed?

Fountain Valley arrests are processed at the FVPD Jail at 10200 Slater Avenue. Misdemeanor bonds post here with 1–3 hour release typical. Felonies and overflow transfer to the Orange County IRC at 550 N Flower Street in Santa Ana.

How much does a bail bond actually cost in Fountain Valley?

The bail bond fee is 10% of the total bail amount under California Insurance Code § 1800.4. For a first-offense DUI with bail set at $15,000, you pay $1,500. That 10% is our fee for posting the full bond — it is not refunded at case conclusion. Payment plans are available for qualifying clients.

Can Fountain Valley bail be posted the same night?

Yes, in most cases. California does not impose a mandatory overnight hold. Once the booking process is complete and the Orange County bail schedule amount is assigned, a licensed bondsman can post the bond immediately. Most Fountain Valley DUI and misdemeanor arrests result in same-night or early-morning release.
